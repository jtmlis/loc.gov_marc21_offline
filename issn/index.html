||About the U.S. ISSN Center||

The U.S. ISSN Center, part of the international ISSN Network, assigns International Standard Serial Numbers (ISSN) to serials published in the U.S. The ISSN is a standard identifier for serials (e.g., journals, magazines, newsletters, newspapers, annuals) whether published in print, online or other media. Each medium version is assigned a separate ISSN.

||New: November 23rd||

The U.S. ISSN Center has a new ISSN request system, "ISSN Uplink."

With an ISSN Uplink account you can:

•View and store your current and prior requests

•Apply and submit applications online

•Add team members to your account and copy them on communications

•Track the progress of an application

•View all communications related to a specific application in one place

•Have a trackable history of all applications submitted and ISSN assigned

For further information, please review the ISSN Uplink Fast FAQs
